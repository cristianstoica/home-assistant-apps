How scoring works

Skill measures how much better a feed does than a simple baseline. For temperature and wind the baseline is persistence — "tomorrow will be like today". A skill of 0 means the feed is no better than that baseline; higher is better, and a negative skill means it does worse.

Precipitation is scored differently: instead of persistence, its skill compares the feed against chance at calling rain vs. dry days (the Equitable Threat Score). A skill of 0 there means no better than chance.

Confidence. A feed is only ranked once it has both enough verified forecasts (at least the minimum sample count) and a skill score that can actually be computed. Meeting the sample count alone is not enough — some samples can't produce a comparable skill (for example, when there is nothing to compare against), and those feeds are withheld rather than ranked.

Win rate is a simpler, head-to-head view: of the days every feed covered, how often was this feed the closest to what was observed.

Composite blends skill across variables into a single overall ranking, so you can compare feeds at a glance.

Window. "Last N days" scores only recent forecasts, so it reflects how a feed is doing now; "All time" uses every verified forecast on record.
